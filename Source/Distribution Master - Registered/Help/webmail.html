What is Webmail?
    There are a countless number of Webmail services available that provide access to an email account. Two excellent examples are Hotmail's and Yahoo!'s email services.

    These are just two examples of a truely large number of Webmail services. Each having their own advantages and drawbacks. Some are preferred for their convenience, others are preferred for the anonymity that they provide, while others are preferred for a plethora of different reasons.

3D Mail Effects' support for Webmail
    With so many Webmail services available (and more springing up all the time) there's only one way to keep up with it: Webmail scripts.
Webmail Scripts
    Webmail scripts allow 3DME to use virtually any Webmail service, even those services that don't exist yet. This is possible because the functionality of Webmail support within 3DME is exposed through the use of Webmail scripts. As scripts are created (either by myself or by users like yourself) they can be downloaded and added to 3DME without needing to upgrade the program.
Where do I get these scripts?
    Anybody that writes a compliant webmail script (see the Webmail script documentation for a definition of what a compliant Webmail script is) can submit it to me at support@FluidStudios.com and I'll include it with the next patch or update to 3DME.

    You may also check the 3D Mail Effects Website for possible places to find these scripts as they become available.
