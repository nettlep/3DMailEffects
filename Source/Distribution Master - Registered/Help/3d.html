3D Mail Effects gets its name from the 3D Objects you'll find hovering on your desktop when new mail arrives (provided you've configured 3DME properly -- see the Alarm preferences.) By now, you know what they should look like, but just in case, here's a snapshot:

There are variations on this theme -- different 3D models (the letter 'e', a teapot, etc.) and different surface materials (the popular gold, dark silver, chrome red, purple gloss, etc.)

For information on creating your own Models & Materials, visit the Skins section of this documentation.

If you wish to modify the properties of this 3D object, simply right-click on it for a context menu:

You'll find the following functionality exposed by this context menu:

      [count] polygons -- This will probably be of little interest to most people. If you aren't familar with the term 'polygon' in reference to 3D models, don't worry yourself about it. It is simply a measurement of the complexity of the model.

      Load a new model -- This allows you to browse the other models available for display. Note that the models are stored in the following directory:

            <3DME Installation Directory>\Models

      Load a new material -- This allows you to browse the other materials available for display. Note that the materials are stored in the following directory:

            <3DME Installation Directory>\Materials

      Half-size (0.5x) -- Changes the size of the model to its smallest size

      Normal-size (1x) -- Changes the size of the model to its normal size

      Double-size (2x) -- Changes the size of the model to its largest size

      Stay on top -- Causes the 3D popup to remain the top-most window. It will never be hidden behind any other window.

      Close on click -- If selected, the model will close after simply clicking on it. Note that there may be a small delay (less than a second) between the time you click on the model, and the time it actually closes.

      Run email client on click -- If selected, your default email client will be run after simply clicking on the model. Note that there may be a small delay between the time you click on the model, and the time when your email client starts up.

      Close on double-click -- If selected, the model will close after simply double-clicking on it.

      Run email client on double-click -- If selected, your default email client will be run after simply double-clicking on the model. Note that there may be a small delay between the time you click on the model, and the time when your email client starts up.

      Run your default email client -- This simply runs your default email client.

      Help -- You're looking at it.

      Close -- Put simply, this gets rid of the 3D model (until it is summoned again by you or an alarm, etc.)
